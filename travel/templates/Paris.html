Paris

Discover the City of Light with its iconic architecture, world-class museums, charming neighborhoods, and renowned culinary scene.

About Paris

Paris, the capital of France, is one of the world's most visited cities, known for its stunning architecture, art museums, fashion, and gastronomy. Situated on the Seine River, the city is divided into 20 arrondissements (districts), each with its own character and charm. With a history spanning over 2,000 years, Paris grew from a small Celtic settlement called Lutetia to become an influential center for art, culture, and ideas. The city underwent major redesign during the 19th century under Baron Haussmann, creating the wide boulevards and uniform buildings that characterize much of Paris today. Home to approximately 2.1 million residents within the city proper and over 12 million in the greater metropolitan area, Paris continues to be a global hub for fashion, cuisine, art, and culture. The city hosts numerous international events and serves as headquarters for many international organizations, including UNESCO.

Top Attractions

Eiffel Tower

Paris's iconic symbol, offering panoramic views of the city from its three observation levels and sparkling light shows in the evening.

Louvre Museum

The world's largest art museum, housing over 38,000 objects including the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and Winged Victory of Samothrace.

Notre-Dame Cathedral

Medieval Catholic cathedral on the Île de la Cité, known for its French Gothic architecture, stained glass, and flying buttresses.

Montmartre

Bohemian hilltop district crowned by the white-domed Sacré-Cœur Basilica, with winding streets, artists' studios, and spectacular city views.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Parisian adventure

Best Time to Visit

The ideal times to visit Paris are spring (April to June) and fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller than in summer. Spring brings blooming gardens and pleasant temperatures between 11-20°C, perfect for outdoor cafés and park visits. Fall offers beautiful foliage, cultural events, and harvest-inspired cuisine. Summer (July-August) is peak tourist season with temperatures around 15-25°C, longer daylight hours, but also larger crowds and some local businesses closed for vacation. Winter (December-February) is the low season with temperatures ranging from 2-8°C, occasional rain or light snow, but features beautiful holiday decorations, fewer tourists, and lower hotel rates. Consider planning around major events like Paris Fashion Week or the French Open, which can affect accommodation availability.

Transportation

Paris has an excellent public transportation system. The Métro is the fastest way to get around with 16 lines covering most of the city. RER trains connect central Paris with suburbs and major sites like Disneyland Paris and Versailles. Buses offer more scenic routes with views of the city. For convenience, purchase a Navigo Découverte card or Paris Visite pass for unlimited travel. Taxis and ride-sharing services are available but can be slow during rush hour. Bicycles are popular with the Vélib' bike-sharing program offering stations throughout the city. Many attractions are close together, making walking an excellent option for exploring neighborhoods. When arriving from Charles de Gaulle or Orly airports, consider the RER B train or dedicated airport buses for the best combination of speed and value.

Local Cuisine

Parisian cuisine offers everything from Michelin-starred restaurants to cozy neighborhood bistros. For breakfast, enjoy a croissant or pain au chocolat with café au lait at a local boulangerie. Traditional bistro dishes include steak-frites, coq au vin, beef bourguignon, and French onion soup. Visit cheese shops for a selection of over 400 varieties of French cheeses. Street food options include crêpes, both sweet and savory. Pastries and desserts are art forms in Paris—try macarons, éclairs, and Paris-Brest at renowned pâtisseries. For the full experience, visit a traditional brasserie with its lively atmosphere and classic menu. Wine is integral to French dining; even simple restaurants offer quality selections. Fixed-price menus (prix fixe or formule) offer good value. Lunch is typically served from 12-2pm and dinner from 7:30-10pm, with many kitchens closed between service times.

Cultural Etiquette

Always greet shopkeepers, restaurant staff, and other service personnel with "Bonjour" (or "Bonsoir" in the evening) when entering establishments. Learning a few basic French phrases is appreciated, even if your pronunciation isn't perfect. Parisians typically dress more formally than tourists—avoid athletic wear and opt for smart-casual attire when dining out. Speaking quietly in public spaces, especially on public transportation, is considered respectful. Tipping is not obligatory as service is included in prices, but rounding up the bill or leaving a few euros for good service is appreciated. When dining, keep hands visible on the table, not in your lap. Wait to be seated at restaurants rather than seating yourself. Photography may be restricted in some museums and churches—always check for signs. The French appreciate directness but also politeness; saying "please" (s'il vous plaît) and "thank you" (merci) goes a long way.

Paris travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

What are the public transportation options in Paris?

Paris public transportation options: 1) Metro - 16 lines covering most tourist areas; runs 5:30am-1:15am (2:15am weekends); single ticket €2.10, book of 10 (carnet) €16.90. 2) RER - express train network connecting suburbs and airport; uses same tickets as metro within central Paris (zones 1-2). 3) Buses - extensive network with 64 routes; same tickets as metro; useful for scenic travel between attractions. 4) Trams - primarily serve outer districts; same ticketing system. 5) Navigo Découverte pass - weekly (Monday-Sunday) unlimited travel card for €30 plus €5 for the card itself; requires photo. 6) Paris Visite pass - tourist travel card for 1-5 days (€14-€70 depending on zones/duration) with some attraction discounts. 7) Velib' bike share - 15,000 bicycles at 1,400 stations; €5/day or €15/week pass plus usage fees. Single tickets are valid for one journey including connections (metro-to-metro or bus-to-bus, not metro-to-bus) within 90 minutes. Validate tickets in machines before boarding trains or upon entering buses. Transport apps: RATP (official) or Citymapper for navigation.

What are the best hotel options in Paris?

Paris hotel recommendations by area: 1) Le Marais (4th) - Hotel Duo, Hôtel Jeanne d'Arc, or Roi de Sicile for trendy neighborhood with shops, restaurants, and central location; €150-300/night. 2) Saint-Germain-des-Prés (6th) - Hotel Left Bank, Hotel des Marronniers, or Hotel d'Aubusson for classic Paris charm near cafés and Luxembourg Gardens; €180-400/night. 3) Eiffel Tower area (7th) - Hotel Le Walt, Hotel Muguet, or Hôtel La Comtesse for iconic views and upscale residential atmosphere; €160-350/night. 4) Montmartre (18th) - Hotel des Arts, Maison Lepic, or Terrass Hotel for bohemian atmosphere and panoramic views; €120-250/night. 5) Opéra/Grands Boulevards (9th) - Hotel Chopin, Hôtel 34B, or Adèle & Jules for shopping and theaters; €140-280/night. 6) Canal Saint-Martin (10th) - Le Citizen, Hotel Providence, or Hotel du Nord for hip, less touristy neighborhood; €120-230/night. Budget accommodations (€80-150): Generator Paris, Ibis Paris Bastille, The People Hostel. Book 3-6 months ahead for high season (April-October). Most Paris hotels occupy historic buildings with smaller rooms than international standards; request upper floors for better light and potentially Eiffel Tower views.

What are some recommended day trips from Paris?

Recommended Paris day trips: 1) Versailles - majestic royal palace and gardens; 30 minutes by RER C train; €20 entrance (plus gardens €10 on fountain show days); book skip-the-line tickets in advance; closed Mondays. 2) Giverny - Monet's house and gardens with famous water lilies; 45 minutes by train from Saint-Lazare to Vernon plus shuttle bus; €11 entrance; open April-November. 3) Champagne region (Reims/Epernay) - champagne house tours and Gothic cathedral; 45 minutes by TGV train; book cellar tours in advance (Moët & Chandon, Veuve Clicquot, Taittinger from €25). 4) Fontainebleau - historic château and forest; 40 minutes by train from Gare de Lyon; €13 entrance; less crowded alternative to Versailles. 5) Chantilly - castle, art collection, and renowned equestrian center; 25 minutes by train from Gare du Nord; €17 entrance. 6) Disneyland Paris - two theme parks; 35 minutes by RER A train; €56-82 single park tickets, cheaper online in advance. 7) Loire Valley châteaux - guided tours available (€150-200) to Chambord and Chenonceau; independent travel requires early start (1-2 hour train journey). Best organized day trips: Versailles, Giverny, and Champagne region have plentiful tour options if you prefer guided experiences (€60-150).

What are some recommended restaurants in Paris?

Recommended Paris restaurants: 1) Classic French bistros - Bistrot Paul Bert (11th), Le Comptoir du Relais (6th), Chez Georges (2nd), and Allard (6th) offer traditional dishes like steak frites and coq au vin; €30-50 per person. 2) Modern French - Septime (11th), Frenchie (2nd), and Virtus (12th) feature contemporary takes on French cuisine; €40-80 per person; reserve weeks/months ahead. 3) Budget options - Bouillon Chartier (multiple locations), Les Philosophes (4th), and L'As du Fallafel (4th) provide quality meals under €20. 4) Splurge experiences - Le Jules Verne (Eiffel Tower), Le Cinq (Four Seasons), and Epicure (Bristol) offer fine dining from €150-300+ per person. 5) Local favorites - Au Petit Tonneau (7th), Chez Janou (3rd), and La Fontaine de Mars (7th) provide authentic Parisian atmosphere. 6) Street food - Rue des Rosiers (Jewish quarter), Rue Mouffetard market street, and Marché d'Aligre for casual eats and picnic supplies. For best value, order the fixed-price "menu" (often lunch) rather than à la carte. Most restaurants require reservations, easily made online through LaFourchette.com or TheFork app. Lunch service typically 12-2pm; dinner from 7:30-10pm; many restaurants closed Sundays and/or Mondays.

How can I purchase tickets for Paris attractions?

Paris attraction ticket options: 1) Eiffel Tower - official website (tour-eiffel.fr) offers timed tickets 60 days in advance (€18-28 depending on level/access); book 2-3 months ahead for high season visits. 2) Louvre - online tickets (€17) provide timed entry and avoid long ticket lines; free first Saturday evening monthly; closed Tuesdays. 3) Musée d'Orsay - online tickets (€16) with timed entry; reduced rate after 4:45pm (€12); closed Mondays. 4) Notre-Dame Cathedral - currently closed for restoration; forecourt viewable without tickets. 5) Sainte-Chapelle and Conciergerie - joint ticket available online (€15); morning visits recommended to experience stained glass in optimal light. 6) Catacombs - strictly limited capacity requires advance purchase (€29 online vs. €14 at door) to avoid 2+ hour waits. 7) Museum passes - Paris Museum Pass (€52/2 days, €66/4 days, €78/6 days) covers 50+ museums/monuments with skip-the-line access (except Eiffel Tower); available at participating museums or tourist offices. 8) Seine River cruises - multiple companies offer 1-hour sightseeing cruises (€15-20); Bateaux Mouches and Vedettes du Pont Neuf are popular options. Digital tickets on smartphones accepted at most attractions. Consider mid-week and early morning/evening visits to major sites to avoid peak crowds.

What are the lounge options at CDG airport?

Charles de Gaulle (CDG) airport lounge options: 1) Terminal 1: Star Alliance Lounge (for Star Alliance Gold members and business class passengers) and Icare Lounge (accepts Priority Pass, €45 for walk-ins) offer food, drinks, WiFi, and shower facilities. 2) Terminal 2A: Air France Lounge (SkyTeam passengers) and YotelAir Paris (bookable by hour from €59, not technically a lounge but offers private rooms). 3) Terminal 2C: Star Alliance Lounge features buffet dining and comfortable workspaces. 4) Terminal 2D: Salon Air France for SkyTeam passengers. 5) Terminal 2E: Hall K, L, and M each house premium Air France lounges for long-haul business/first class; Hall L contains the exceptional La Première Lounge for first class passengers. 6) Terminal 2F: Air France Business Lounge for short/medium-haul flights. 7) Terminal 3: No dedicated lounges available. Access methods include: airline status/premium tickets, Priority Pass membership, Lounge Pass/DragonPass programs, or walk-up payment (€30-50 depending on lounge). Most lounges open 6am-10pm with basic buffet food, alcoholic/non-alcoholic beverages, WiFi, newspapers, and seating; premium lounges add restaurant dining, spa services, and superior amenities. Check terminal transfer times if connecting flights operate from different terminals (can require 30-60 minutes).

What are family-friendly accommodation options in Paris?

Family-friendly Paris accommodations: 1) Apartment rentals - platforms like Airbnb, VRBO, and Paris Perfect offer multi-bedroom options with kitchens; most economical for families of 4+; typically €150-300/night depending on location and size; Marais, Saint-Germain, and 7th arrondissement provide central locations. 2) Family-oriented hotels - Novotel Paris Les Halles, Residence Henri IV, and Hotel la Bourdonnais offer dedicated family rooms or connecting options; €180-300/night; many include kitchenettes. 3) Aparthotels - Citadines, Adagio, and Staycity combine hotel services with apartment amenities; €150-250/night; ideal for longer stays with weekly rates. 4) Budget options - Ibis Styles, MIJE hostels (private family rooms available), and Generator Paris accommodate families for €100-180/night. 5) Luxury family stays - Le Bristol, Four Seasons George V, and Peninsula Paris offer dedicated children's programs, welcome amenities for kids, and babysitting services; €500+/night. Consider accommodations near Luxembourg Gardens, Jardin des Plantes, or Tuileries for easy access to play areas. Request specific arrangements when booking: baby equipment (cribs, high chairs), refrigerators for storing milk/snacks, and confirmation of elevator access as many historic buildings have limited or no elevators. Book 4-6 months ahead for family rooms, which are limited in many Paris hotels.

Where can I purchase a Navigo Découverte pass in Paris?

Navigo Découverte pass purchase locations: 1) Metro/RER station ticket offices (not all stations have staffed windows; larger stations like Gare du Nord, Châtelet, Saint-Lazare, and Montparnasse are most reliable). 2) RATP information centers at major stations including Gare de Lyon, Charles de Gaulle Airport Terminal 2, and Les Halles. 3) Some tobacconists (Tabac shops with the brown/red "tabac" signs) sell and recharge Navigo cards. The pass costs €5 for the reloadable card itself plus the weekly fare (€30 for all zones 1-5 including airports) or monthly fare (€90). Requirements: small ID photo (3.5 x 4.1 cm) needed for the card; many stations have photo booths (€5 for 4 photos) or bring one with you. You must write your name on the card and attach the photo. Weekly passes (Navigo Semaine) run Monday-Sunday regardless of purchase day; monthly passes (Navigo Mois) are valid for calendar months. When purchasing, specify "toutes zones" (all zones) if you need airport access (zones 1-5) or just central Paris (zones 1-2) if not. Recharging for subsequent weeks can be done at any ticket vending machine. Alternative for short stays: Paris Visite tourist pass (1-5 days) or carnets (books of 10 individual tickets) require no photo and are more flexible for shorter visits.
