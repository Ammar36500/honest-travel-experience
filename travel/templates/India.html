India

Experience the kaleidoscope of cultures, ancient traditions, vibrant colors, diverse landscapes, and rich history in this land of contrasts.

About India

India, officially the Republic of India, is a country in South Asia and the world's most populous nation with over 1.4 billion people. Bounded by the Indian Ocean, Arabian Sea, and Bay of Bengal, India shares borders with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. This ancient civilization dates back to the Indus Valley (3300-1300 BCE) and has been shaped by diverse empires and kingdoms. India gained independence from British rule in 1947 and is now the world's largest democracy. The country's geography varies dramatically from the Himalayan mountains to tropical beaches and desert regions. India is known for its cultural diversity with 22 officially recognized languages and hundreds of dialects, various religions including Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, and Jainism, and distinctive regional cultures. The nation has a rapidly growing economy that blends traditional village farming, modern agriculture, and a wide range of industrial and technological sectors. India's contributions to mathematics, astronomy, art, literature, philosophy, and spiritual traditions have influenced global culture. From bustling metropolises like Mumbai and Delhi to serene spiritual centers like Varanasi, India offers visitors an unparalleled range of experiences.

Top Attractions

Taj Mahal

Iconic white marble mausoleum in Agra, built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, exemplifying Mughal architecture at its finest.

Varanasi

One of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, with sacred ghats along the Ganges River where spiritual ceremonies take place.

Jaipur

Rajasthan's "Pink City" featuring stunning palaces, forts, and vibrant bazaars that showcase the region's royal heritage.

Kerala Backwaters

Serene network of canals, lakes, and lagoons parallel to the Arabian Sea coast, best explored via traditional houseboat.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Indian adventure

Best Time to Visit

October to March is generally the best time to visit most of India, with mild, dry weather across the country. The summer months (April to June) bring extreme heat to most regions, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C. Monsoon season (June to September) brings heavy rainfall to most parts of the country, though the intensity varies by region. For the Himalayan regions like Ladakh and parts of Himachal Pradesh, summer months (May to September) offer the best conditions as winter can bring heavy snowfall and closed passes. For wildlife viewing and tiger safaris, visit between November and May when vegetation is thinner and animals gather around water sources. If attending specific festivals like Holi (March), Diwali (October/November), or Pushkar Camel Fair (November), plan your visit accordingly.

Transportation

India's railway network is extensive and affordable, connecting most cities and towns—advance booking is recommended through the IRCTC website or app. Domestic flights offer time-saving options between major destinations, with numerous budget carriers available. In cities, app-based services like Uber and Ola provide convenient alternatives to traditional auto-rickshaws and taxis. Metro systems in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Chennai, and other major cities offer efficient ways to avoid road traffic. For intercity travel, government and private buses operate extensive routes, ranging from basic to luxury sleeper coaches. Auto-rickshaws and cycle-rickshaws are common for short distances in cities and towns—always agree on the fare before starting your journey. For remote areas, consider hiring a car with driver, which offers flexibility and local knowledge. Traffic moves on the left side of the road, and driving conditions can be challenging for foreigners—most visitors avoid self-driving.

Local Cuisine

Indian cuisine varies dramatically by region, from the rich, creamy curries of Punjab to the coconut-infused dishes of Kerala. Street food is an essential part of Indian culinary culture—try local specialties like pani puri, vada pav, dosas, and chaat from vendors with high turnover and good hygiene practices. Vegetarian options are abundant throughout the country, with nearly 40% of Indians following vegetarian diets. Non-vegetarians should try butter chicken in Delhi, fish curry in Goa, and kebabs in Lucknow. Thali meals offer an excellent sampling of regional flavors, typically including rice, bread, multiple curries, and condiments. Traditional beverages include masala chai (spiced tea), lassi (yogurt drink), and fresh fruit juices. Spice levels vary widely—don't hesitate to request mild preparation if you're sensitive to spicy food. When dining out, service charges are often included in the bill; additional tipping is appreciated but not mandatory.

Cultural Etiquette

When visiting temples, mosques, or other religious sites, remove your shoes and dress modestly, covering shoulders and knees. The traditional greeting is "Namaste" with palms pressed together. Public displays of affection are generally frowned upon. Eat with your right hand only if dining traditionally without utensils, as the left hand is considered unclean. Ask permission before photographing people, especially in rural areas and at religious ceremonies. Bargaining is expected in markets, but not in fixed-price shops—start at about 50% of the initial asking price. Modest dress is recommended, particularly for women traveling in more conservative regions. When entering homes,temples, or other private spaces, removing your shoes is customary. Indians often communicate through head movements—a side-to-side wobble typically means "yes" or agreement. Many Indians speak English, particularly in urban and tourist areas, but learning a few basic phrases in the local language is appreciated. When accepting gifts, money, or business cards, use your right hand or both hands. Conversation topics about family are welcome, but avoid political discussions, particularly regarding border disputes or religious tensions. Be respectful when discussing India's social issues and challenges.

India travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

What are some recommended itineraries for traveling in India?

Popular India itineraries include: 1) North India Classics (10-14 days) - Delhi, Agra (Taj Mahal), Jaipur, Varanasi, and optional Amritsar or Rishikesh. 2) South India (12-15 days) - Mumbai, Goa beaches, Kerala backwaters, Kochi, and Hampi ruins. 3) Himalayan Adventure (10-14 days) - Delhi, Shimla, Dharamshala, Manali, with potential extension to Ladakh (summer only). 4) Northeast Explorer (14+ days) - Kolkata, Darjeeling, Gangtok, and Kaziranga National Park. 5) Religious Circuit (10 days) - Varanasi, Bodh Gaya, Rishikesh, and Haridwar. First-time visitors typically choose North India for iconic landmarks. The best travel seasons are October-March (dry, mild weather), avoiding summer monsoons (June-September) in most regions. Plan 2-3 nights per major city and include travel buffer days as transportation can be unpredictable. Consider domestic flights for longer distances to maximize sightseeing time.

What are recommended itineraries for a Rajasthan tour?

Rajasthan itineraries include: 1) Essential Rajasthan (7 days) - Jaipur (Pink City, Amber Fort), Jodhpur (Mehrangarh Fort, blue buildings), and Udaipur (lakes, City Palace). 2) Extended Route (10-12 days) - adds Jaisalmer (desert fortress, dune safari), Pushkar (sacred lake, Brahma Temple), and Ranthambore National Park (tigers). 3) Off-beat Rajasthan (14 days) - includes lesser-visited Bundi (stepwells), Bikaner (rat temple), and Shekhawati (painted havelis). October-March offers ideal weather; avoid May-June (extreme heat reaching 45°C). Consider hiring a driver (₹3500-5000/day including vehicle, fuel, driver accommodation) as trains/buses can be crowded. Palace hotels (converted royal residences starting at ₹6000/night) offer unique cultural experiences, while heritage havelis provide mid-range options (₹2000-4000/night). Include a desert safari for experiencing rural life, traditional music, and sunset camel rides.

What are the requirements for an e-visa to India?

India e-visa requirements include: 1) Passport with minimum 6 months validity and at least two blank pages. 2) Recent 2x2 inch color photograph with white background. 3) Return/onward ticket confirmation. 4) Proof of sufficient funds for your stay. 5) Valid email address and credit/debit card for payment. The e-visa application must be submitted at least 4 days before travel through the official portal (indianvisaonline.gov.in). E-visas are available as: Tourist e-visa (30 days: $25, 1 year: $40, 5 years: $80), Business e-visa ($80), or Medical e-visa ($80). Processing typically takes 2-4 business days. E-visa holders must enter through designated airports/seaports. Double-check entry points compatibility before booking flights. The e-visa is non-extendable and non-convertible to other visa types.

What are the travel options between Delhi and Agra?

Delhi-Agra travel options (230km) include: 1) Trains - fastest and most popular choice with options like Gatimaan Express (India's fastest train, 1hr 40min, ₹800-1500), Shatabdi Express (2hrs, ₹800-1200), or cheaper passenger trains (3-4hrs, ₹200-300). 2) Private car/taxi - flexible option allowing stops at Mathura or Fatehpur Sikri (₹3500-5000 for full day). 3) Bus - AC Volvo services from Kashmere Gate ISBT (4hrs, ₹700-1000) or UP State buses (5hrs, ₹450-600). 4) Group tours - one-day excursions from Delhi (₹2000-4000) including transportation and guide. 5) Uber/Ola intercity services available (₹3000-3500). Book trains 1-2 months ahead through IRCTC website/app, especially for morning departures. If visiting Taj Mahal, remember it's closed on Fridays. Most travelers prefer morning departure from Delhi, afternoon at Taj Mahal, and either return same day or overnight in Agra.

What are popular itineraries for India's Golden Triangle tours?

Golden Triangle itineraries include: 1) Classic (6-7 days) - Delhi (2 days): Red Fort, Jama Masjid, Humayun's Tomb; Agra (1 day): Taj Mahal, Agra Fort; Jaipur (2-3 days): Amber Fort, City Palace, Hawa Mahal. 2) Extended (8-10 days) - adds Ranthambore National Park for tiger safaris or Pushkar for its sacred lake. 3) Luxury (7 days) - using 5-star hotels and Palace on Wheels train. 4) Budget backpacker (6-8 days) - using public transportation and guesthouses (total ₹15000-20000 excluding flights). Transportation options include pre-arranged car with driver (most convenient, ₹4000-5000/day), trains (most scenic, economical), or guided group tours (hassle-free, ₹20000-40000 all-inclusive). October-March offers ideal weather; avoid June-August (monsoon season). Add buffer days as driving times can be longer than expected due to traffic and road conditions.

What are the requirements for an Indian eVisa?

Indian eVisa requirements include: 1) Digital passport-size photograph (square, white background, 350x350 pixels). 2) Passport scan (PDF, front and back pages) valid for 6+ months with two blank pages. 3) Valid email and credit/debit card. 4) Additional documents depending on visa type: Business (business card, company letter); Medical (hospital letter); Conference (invitation). Apply through indianvisaonline.gov.in 4-30 days before travel. Tourist eVisas come in three durations: 30-day ($25), 1-year ($40), and 5-year ($80). Double-entry allowed for 30-day eVisas; multiple entries for longer durations. Maximum stay is 90 days per visit (180 days for US, UK, Canada, and Japan citizens). Travelers must enter through designated 29 airports or 5 seaports. The visa becomes valid from your date of arrival, not application approval date. E-visas cannot be extended or converted to other visa types within India.

What are common issues with India's e-Visa application process?

Common e-Visa issues include: 1) Photo/document format problems - photos must be square (350x350 pixels), color, full-face, white background; documents must be clear PDFs under 3MB. 2) Payment failures - use Visa/Mastercard and enable international transactions before applying. 3) Website glitches - clear browser cookies, try different browsers, or apply during off-peak hours. 4) Application number loss - keep multiple copies of your temporary application ID. 5) Name format issues - enter exactly as shown in passport, including middle names. 6) Wrong visa type selection - carefully review restrictions before choosing. 7) Missing entry points - verify your port of entry is e-Visa compatible. If your application is "Under Process" for over 72 hours, email support at indiatvoa@gov.in with your application ID. Avoid third-party websites charging extra fees; always use the official indianvisaonline.gov.in site. Apply at least 1 week before travel to allow time for resolving potential issues.

What are the travel options to Goa from Mumbai?

Mumbai-Goa travel options include: 1) Flights - fastest option (1 hour) with multiple daily departures from airlines like IndiGo, SpiceJet, and Air India (₹2000-5000 one-way). 2) Trains - most scenic route along the Konkan coast with options including Tejas Express (premium service, 8 hours, ₹1200-2000), Jan Shatabdi (8.5 hours, ₹700-900), or overnight sleepers like Konkan Kanya Express (10-12 hours, ₹500-1800 depending on class). 3) Buses - luxury sleeper coaches operated by MSRTC and private companies (10-12 hours, ₹800-2000). 4) Self-drive - 590km journey via NH-66 (10-12 hours) with scenic coastal views but variable road conditions. 5) Ferry services - seasonal catamarans operate between Mumbai and North Goa (February-May, 7 hours, ₹4000-5000). Book trains 2-3 months ahead through IRCTC as they fill quickly, especially during October-March peak season. Most trains arrive at Madgaon (South Goa) or Thivim (North Goa) stations, requiring additional local transport to beach areas.
