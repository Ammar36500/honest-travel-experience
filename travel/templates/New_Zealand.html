New Zealand

Discover breathtaking landscapes, vibrant Māori culture, and thrilling adventures in the land of the long white cloud.

About New Zealand

New Zealand is an island nation in the southwestern Pacific Ocean known for its stunning landscapes, ranging from fjords and mountains to beaches and rolling farmlands. It's renowned for its indigenous Māori culture, diverse outdoor activities, Lord of the Rings filming locations, and commitment to environmental conservation. The country is made up of two main islands - the North Island (Te Ika-a-Māui) and the South Island (Te Waipounamu) - along with many smaller islands.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Kiwi adventure

Best Time to Visit

December to February (summer) for beaches and hiking; June to August (winter) for skiing and snow activities.

Transportation

Rent a car or campervan for flexibility. Remember they drive on the left side of the road. Bus networks connect major towns.

Local Cuisine

Try hāngī (traditional Māori cooking method), lamb, green-lipped mussels, hokey pokey ice cream, and excellent local wines.

Eco-Tourism

Follow the "Tiaki Promise" - care for land, sea and nature, treading lightly and leaving no trace.

New Zealand travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

What are some self-drive itinerary suggestions for New Zealand?

Popular self-drive itineraries: 1) Classic 14-day North & South - Auckland to Rotorua (2 days), Tongariro (1), Wellington (2), Picton/Marlborough (1), Kaikoura (1), Christchurch (1), Lake Tekapo (1), Queenstown (3), Milford Sound (1), returning to Christchurch. 2) South Island Highlights (10 days) - Christchurch to Lake Tekapo, Mt. Cook, Wanaka, Queenstown, Milford Sound, Te Anau, returning via West Coast (Franz Josef Glacier) and Arthur's Pass. 3) North Island Circuit (7 days) - Auckland, Bay of Islands, Coromandel Peninsula, Rotorua, Taupo, Napier, Wellington. 4) South Island Adventure (14 days) - Christchurch, Kaikoura, Abel Tasman, West Coast, Wanaka, Queenstown, Mt. Cook, Dunedin, Catlins, returning to Christchurch. Best seasons: December-February (summer) for reliable weather; March-May and September-November (shoulder seasons) for fewer crowds and better rates. Allow more driving time than GPS estimates - winding roads and photo stops extend journey times by 25-50%. Pre-book accommodation 3-6 months ahead for peak season (December-February).

What is a recommended New Zealand North Island itinerary?

Recommended 7-10 day North Island itinerary: Day 1-2: Auckland - explore harbor, Sky Tower, nearby Waiheke Island for wineries. Day 3-4: Bay of Islands - drive north (3hrs) for maritime activities, Waitangi Treaty Grounds, and dolphin watching. Day 5: Coromandel Peninsula - Hot Water Beach (dig your own spa) and Cathedral Cove coastal walk. Day 6-7: Rotorua - geothermal attractions (Wai-O-Tapu, Te Puia), Maori cultural experiences (Tamaki Maori Village), and adventure activities. Day 8: Taupo - visit Huka Falls and geothermal parks. Day 9: Tongariro National Park - Alpine Crossing day hike (among world's best day walks) or shorter walks if weather poor. Day 10: Wellington - Te Papa museum, Cuba Street, and waterfront. With extra days, add Napier (Art Deco architecture and wineries), Hobbiton Movie Set near Matamata, or Waitomo Glowworm Caves. Best base cities for multiple activities: Rotorua, Taupo, and Paihia (Bay of Islands). Driving distances are manageable (mostly 2-4 hours between major stops) but allow extra time for scenic stops and winding roads.

What are must-see places in New Zealand?

New Zealand must-see places: 1) Milford Sound - dramatic fiord with waterfalls and peaks; day trips from Queenstown or overnight cruises. 2) Rotorua - geothermal wonderland with geysers, mud pools, and Maori cultural experiences. 3) Queenstown - adventure capital offering bungy jumping, jet boating, and hiking amid stunning mountain scenery. 4) Bay of Islands - subtropical maritime paradise with 144 islands, perfect for sailing and dolphin spotting. 5) Tongariro National Park - volcanic landscapes featuring the Tongariro Alpine Crossing day hike. 6) Abel Tasman National Park - golden beaches and turquoise waters ideal for kayaking and coastal walking. 7) Franz Josef & Fox Glaciers - accessible glaciers descending into temperate rainforest. 8) Waitomo Caves - underground network featuring thousands of glowworms. 9) Lake Wanaka & Mount Aspiring National Park - alpine lakes and mountains with hiking and photography opportunities. 10) Kaikoura - marine wildlife including whales, dolphins, and seals against mountain backdrop. Best experienced: October-April for reliable weather; November for spring flowers; February for warm temperatures and less crowded conditions after school holidays.

What are popular travel routes in New Zealand?

Popular New Zealand routes: 1) Classic North to South - Auckland to Queenstown/Christchurch (14-21 days), covering highlights of both islands with Interislander Ferry crossing between Wellington and Picton. 2) South Island Circuit (10-14 days) - clockwise or counterclockwise from Christchurch covering Kaikoura, Marlborough, Abel Tasman, West Coast, Queenstown, and Mount Cook. 3) Southern Scenic Route (5-7 days) - Queenstown to Dunedin via Te Anau, Milford Sound, Invercargill, and the Catlins coastal area. 4) Thermal Explorer Highway (4-7 days) - Auckland to Napier/Hawke's Bay via Hamilton, Rotorua, and Taupo, focusing on geothermal attractions. 5) Twin Coast Discovery (5-7 days) - Auckland loop through Northland, Bay of Islands, and the Kauri Coast. 6) Pacific Coast Highway (5-7 days) - Auckland to Napier via Coromandel, Tauranga, and East Cape's coastal scenery. For international visitors, one-way rentals between Auckland and Christchurch/Queenstown are popular despite one-way fees (NZD 100-350), saving backtracking time. December-March sees heaviest tourist traffic; allow extra time for road construction delays during summer months.

What are the options for campervan rentals in New Zealand?

New Zealand campervan rental options: 1) Major operators: Maui/Britz/Mighty (premium to budget options under same company), Jucy (budget-friendly smaller vehicles), Apollo/Hippie, and Kea (premium). 2) Vehicle types range from sleepervans (modified minivans sleeping 2) to 6-berth motorhomes with shower/toilet facilities. 3) Costs: NZD 50-100/day for basic sleepervans, NZD 150-250/day for 2-4 berth self-contained vehicles, NZD 250-400/day for premium 4-6 berth motorhomes. Peak season (December-February) rates can double; significant discounts for extended rentals and off-season travel. 4) Self-containment certification (onboard toilet) necessary for freedom camping in many areas. 5) Pickup locations in Auckland, Christchurch, Queenstown with one-way rentals available (surcharge applies). 6) Additional costs: insurance excess reduction (NZD 20-45/day), diesel road user charges (approximately NZD 7 per 100km), ferry crossing between islands (NZD 250-450 for campervans). 7) Booking recommendations: reserve 6-9 months ahead for December-February travel; 3-4 months for shoulder seasons. Compare inclusions carefully - companies offering "all inclusive" rates may provide better value despite higher initial quotes.

What should I know about renting a car in New Zealand?

New Zealand car rental essentials: 1) Requirements - full driver's license (English translation/International Driving Permit if not in English), minimum age 21-25 depending on company, credit card for security deposit. 2) Major companies include Avis, Budget, Hertz, Thrifty plus local operators like Apex, GO Rentals, and Jucy offering competitive rates. 3) Costs range from NZD 30-60/day for economy cars to NZD 70-120/day for SUVs/4WDs; longer rentals receive significant discounts. 4) Driving is on the left side; automatic transmissions widely available but cost ~10% more than manual. 5) Insurance options: standard coverage typically has high excess (NZD 2000-4000); excess reduction fees cost NZD 15-30/day but are worth considering. 6) One-way rentals between islands require ferry crossing (vehicle NZD 150-250 plus passenger fares) or separate rentals on each island (more economical for shorter trips). 7) Fuel costs approximately NZD 2.50-3.00/liter; diesel vehicles incur additional Road User Charges (calculated at return). 8) Rural areas have limited fuel stations - fill up when below half tank. 9) Allow extra driving time - GPS estimates underestimate journeys due to winding roads. 10) Book 2-3 months ahead for peak season (December-February); check for "relocation deals" offering $1/day rentals with limited time windows.

What scenic train journeys are available in New Zealand?

New Zealand's scenic train journeys: 1) TranzAlpine (Christchurch to Greymouth) - 4.5 hours crossing the Southern Alps via Arthur's Pass with spectacular mountain, river and forest scenery; widely considered one of world's most beautiful train journeys; NZD 159-209 one-way. 2) Northern Explorer (Auckland to Wellington) - 10.5 hours traversing volcanic plateaus, farmland and coastline; passes through Tongariro National Park with views of Mt. Ruapehu; NZD 159-219 one-way. 3) Coastal Pacific (Picton to Christchurch) - 5.5 hours along the east coast with Pacific Ocean views and Kaikoura mountain ranges; seasonal operation (Sep-Apr); NZD 159-209 one-way. All trains feature open-air viewing carriages and GPS-triggered audio commentary. Premium Plus class offers enhanced catering and wider seats. Advance booking (2-3 months for peak season) highly recommended as trains operate limited schedules (1-7 weekly departures depending on route and season). Stopover options available with Scenic Rail Passes allowing flexible travel within 12-month period. These journeys are experience-focused rather than primary transportation - consider one-way train combined with return car rental for efficient sightseeing.

What are family-friendly activities in New Zealand?

Family-friendly New Zealand activities: 1) Hobbiton Movie Set (Matamata) - guided tours of The Lord of the Rings filming location with child-friendly commentary and scavenger hunts; suitable for all ages. 2) Te Puia (Rotorua) - geothermal park with Pohutu geyser, Maori cultural performances, and kiwi bird viewing. 3) Skyline Gondola and Luge (Queenstown and Rotorua) - scenic rides and gravity-fueled luge tracks with options for various ages. 4) Zealandia Ecosanctuary (Wellington) - wildlife reserve with easy walking trails to spot native birds and reptiles. 5) International Antarctic Centre (Christchurch) - interactive exhibits, penguin encounters, and simulated Antarctic storm experience. 6) Agrodome (Rotorua) - farm show demonstrating sheep shearing and working dogs with hands-on opportunities. 7) Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland (Rotorua) - colorful geothermal pools and walkways suitable for all fitness levels. 8) Mitai Maori Village (Rotorua) - cultural performances and hangi dinner in family-suitable evening format. 9) Animal encounters at Wellington Zoo, Auckland Zoo, and Rainbow Springs (Rotorua) featuring native wildlife. 10) Waitomo Glowworm Caves - boat rides through caves illuminated by thousands of glowworms; suitable for all ages. Most attractions offer family tickets saving 10-20% over individual prices; children 0-4 typically free, 5-15 at reduced rates.
