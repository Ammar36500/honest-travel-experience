Indonesia

Discover the world's largest archipelago with its stunning beaches, ancient temples, lush jungles, and vibrant cultural heritage.

About Indonesia

Indonesia spans over 17,000 islands between the Indian and Pacific Oceans, making it the world's largest archipelago. With over 270 million people, it's the fourth most populous country globally and home to hundreds of ethnic groups speaking more than 700 languages. Its diverse landscapes include pristine beaches, active volcanoes, dense rainforests, and terraced rice fields. Indonesia's rich history shows influences from Hindu, Buddhist, Islamic, and colonial European cultures. The country achieved independence in 1945 and has developed into the largest economy in Southeast Asia, with tourism as a major industry particularly in Bali, Jakarta, and Yogyakarta.

Top Attractions

Bali

Indonesia's most famous island, known for its stunning beaches, terraced rice fields, spiritual temples, vibrant arts scene, and world-class surfing spots.

Borobudur Temple

The world's largest Buddhist temple dating from the 9th century, featuring 504 Buddha statues and intricate relief carvings telling Buddhist stories.

Komodo National Park

Home to the ancient Komodo dragon, pristine beaches, and some of the world's best diving sites with vibrant coral reefs and rich marine biodiversity.

Raja Ampat

An archipelago of 1,500 small islands with the richest marine biodiversity on Earth, featuring pristine beaches, hidden lagoons, and world-class diving.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Indonesian adventure

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (May to September) offers the best travel conditions with sunny days and low humidity, ideal for exploring beaches, temples, and hiking. The wet season (October to April) brings daily rainfall but fewer tourists and lush landscapes. Different regions have varied microclimates - Bali and Java are best visited during dry season, while Raja Ampat's prime diving season is October to April. Cultural events like Nyepi in Bali (March/April) and independence celebrations (August) add unique experiences to your trip.

Transportation

Indonesia's transportation varies widely across islands. Domestic flights connect major destinations and are essential for island hopping. In cities, ride-hailing apps like Grab and GoJek offer convenient transport. Trains operate in Java and Sumatra with comfortable, affordable services. Ferries connect nearby islands, while speedboats serve tourist routes. For local travel, bemos (minivans) and ojeks (motorcycle taxis) are common. On islands like Bali, scooter rentals provide flexibility, though traffic can be chaotic.

Local Cuisine

Indonesian cuisine varies across regions but shares a love for bold flavors. Nasi goreng (fried rice) and mie goreng (fried noodles) are national favorites. Try satay - grilled skewered meat with peanut sauce, and rendang - a rich, slow-cooked beef curry. For the adventurous, sample unique dishes like babi guling (suckling pig) in Bali or spicy Padang cuisine. Warungs (local eateries) offer authentic food at reasonable prices. Street food is abundant but choose busy stalls with high turnover. Fresh tropical fruits and juices are widely available.

Cultural Etiquette

Indonesia is culturally diverse with predominantly Muslim regions. Dress modestly, especially outside tourist areas and when visiting temples. Remove shoes before entering homes or religious sites. Use your right hand for eating and giving or receiving items. Greet people with a gentle handshake and a slight bow. Learn basic phrases like "terima kasih" (thank you). When visiting temples, wear a sarong and sash (often provided at entrances). Avoid public displays of affection. Be respectful when taking photos, especially of people and religious ceremonies.

Indonesia travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

What are must-see places and activities in Indonesia?

Indonesia's top destinations include: 1) Bali - with sacred temples (Uluwatu, Tanah Lot), terraced rice fields (Tegallalang), and beaches (Kuta, Seminyak, Nusa Dua). 2) Yogyakarta - home to ancient temples Borobudur (world's largest Buddhist temple) and Prambanan (Hindu complex). 3) Komodo National Park - for seeing Komodo dragons and world-class diving/snorkeling. 4) Raja Ampat - premier diving destination with unparalleled marine biodiversity. 5) Mount Bromo - active volcano with spectacular sunrise views. 6) Tana Toraja (Sulawesi) - unique funeral ceremonies and traditional villages. 7) Ubud (Bali) - cultural center with monkey forest, art markets, and yoga retreats. 8) Gili Islands - car-free paradise for diving, snorkeling, and relaxing. The dry season (April-October) is optimal for most regions, though eastern Indonesia (Raja Ampat, Maluku) is best visited during the western wet season (November-March).

What are the travel options between cities in Indonesia?

Indonesia travel options include: 1) Flights - most efficient for this archipelago nation, with Garuda Indonesia, Lion Air, AirAsia, and Citilink connecting major islands. Book 1-2 months ahead for best rates (Jakarta-Bali: IDR 700,000-1,200,000). 2) Ferries - essential for shorter inter-island trips, especially between Bali, Lombok, and the Gili Islands (Bali-Lombok fast boats: IDR 250,000-500,000, 1.5-2.5 hours). 3) Trains - available on Java with comfortable, air-conditioned services connecting Jakarta, Yogyakarta, and Surabaya (Jakarta-Yogyakarta: IDR 350,000-500,000, 7-8 hours). 4) Long-distance buses - economical option on major islands (Jakarta-Yogyakarta: IDR 200,000-300,000, 10-12 hours). 5) Ride-hailing - Grab and Gojek apps offer convenient city transportation and short-distance travel. 6) Private drivers - affordable option for day trips or multi-day tours (IDR 500,000-800,000/day including car and driver).

What travel advice is available for Sulawesi, Indonesia?

For Sulawesi travel: 1) Toraja Highlands (South Sulawesi) - allow 3-4 days to explore traditional villages, funeral ceremonies, and cliff burials; hire a local guide for cultural context. 2) Bunaken Marine Park (North Sulawesi) - world-class diving/snorkeling with 70+ coral species and hundreds of fish species; stay in Manado and take daily boat trips. 3) Togean Islands - remote archipelago with pristine beaches and diverse marine life; requires planning as boats run only 2-3 times weekly. 4) Makassar - gateway city with Dutch colonial history and seafood restaurants along Losari Beach. 5) Transportation logistics - rely on flights between major cities (Makassar, Manado, Gorontalo); prepare for limited road quality and long travel times elsewhere. 6) Cultural sensitivity - dress modestly, particularly in rural areas and Muslim communities. 7) Visit during dry season (May-October) for optimal conditions, especially for diving/snorkeling. 8) Security considerations - check current advisories for Central Sulawesi following past natural disasters.

How can I obtain a tourist visa for Indonesia?

Indonesia visa options include: 1) Visa Exemption (Bebas Visa Kunjungan) - free 30-day stay for 160+ nationalities including US, UK, EU, Australia, and Japan; non-extendable and for tourism only. 2) Visa on Arrival (VOA) - available at major entry points for 30-day tourism/business stays (IDR 500,000/~$35); can be extended once for additional 30 days. 3) E-Visa - apply online through Indonesia's immigration website (4-7 business days processing) for tourism, business, or social visits. 4) Embassy/Consulate visas - for stays longer than 60 days or purposes not covered by simpler options. Requirements include passport valid 6+ months beyond arrival date, return/onward tickets, and proof of sufficient funds. Those planning to visit remote areas (Papua) need additional permits. Overstay penalties are IDR 1,000,000 (~$65) per day. Single-entry visas become void once you exit Indonesia, requiring a new visa for re-entry.

Where should I stay when visiting Bali?

Bali accommodation areas include: 1) Seminyak - upscale beach area with designer boutiques, fine dining, and beach clubs; best for couples and luxury travelers (IDR 800,000-3,000,000/night). 2) Kuta - budget-friendly area with nightlife, surfing, and shopping; ideal for first-timers and backpackers (IDR 200,000-800,000/night). 3) Ubud - cultural center surrounded by rice terraces and rainforest; perfect for wellness retreats, art enthusiasts, and nature lovers (IDR 400,000-2,000,000/night). 4) Canggu - hip surfer area with digital nomad scene, rice fields, and beach clubs; great for longer stays (IDR 500,000-1,500,000/night). 5) Nusa Dua - gated resort area with pristine beaches and water sports; family-friendly with minimal street vendors (IDR 1,000,000-4,000,000/night). 6) Uluwatu - clifftop area with dramatic ocean views, surfing spots, and luxury resorts; quieter atmosphere (IDR 800,000-3,000,000/night). 7) Sanur - relaxed beachfront area with calm waters and traditional atmosphere; popular with older travelers and families (IDR 600,000-2,000,000/night).

What are the recommended diving destinations in Indonesia?

Top Indonesian diving destinations: 1) Raja Ampat (West Papua) - world's highest marine biodiversity with 1,500+ fish species, manta rays, and pristine coral; best October-April, expensive but unparalleled (resorts $150-400/night, liveaboards $300-500/day). 2) Komodo National Park - diverse sites featuring manta cleaning stations, reef sharks, and vibrant coral gardens; best April-November when visibility reaches 30m. 3) Bunaken Marine Park (North Sulawesi) - dramatic walls dropping 25-50m with turtles, barracuda, and 70% of all fish species found in Indo-Pacific; year-round destination. 4) Wakatobi (Southeast Sulawesi) - remote location with pristine reefs, walls, and macro life; premium accommodation focusing on responsible tourism. 5) Nusa Penida (near Bali) - famous for mola-mola (sunfish) from July-October and manta rays year-round; suitable for day trips from Bali. 6) Alor and Banda Sea - frontier diving with hammerhead sharks, volcanic seamounts, and untouched reefs; typically accessed via liveaboards. 7) Certification options range from PADI Open Water courses in Bali/Gili Islands ($350-450) to advanced specialty courses at dedicated dive resorts.

What are some travel tips for Flores Island?

Flores travel tips: 1) Transportation - fly to Labuan Bajo (western gateway) from Bali (1 hour) or take 4-day boat trips from Lombok including Komodo National Park visits. 2) Komodo National Park - book day trips from Labuan Bajo to see dragons, hike Padar Island, and snorkel/dive (IDR 800,000-1,500,000/day plus IDR 150,000 park fee); advance booking essential. 3) Kelimutu National Park - visit the tri-colored volcanic lakes early morning for best views; stay in nearby Moni village. 4) Traditional villages - experience authentic cultures in Bena and Wae Rebo villages (requires advance arrangements and modest donations). 5) Infrastructure - prepare for basic facilities, limited ATMs, and spotty internet outside major towns; withdraw cash in Labuan Bajo or Ende. 6) Road travel - allow extra time as the trans-Flores highway is winding and journey times often exceed estimates (Labuan Bajo to Ende: 10-12 hours). 7) Best time to visit: April-November (dry season) for optimal conditions. 8) Accommodation ranges from basic guesthouses in small towns (IDR 200,000-400,000) to boutique resorts in Labuan Bajo (IDR 800,000-3,000,000).

What are recommended travel itineraries for Yogyakarta?

Yogyakarta itineraries include: 1) Essential Yogyakarta (3 days) - Day 1: Borobudur sunrise, Pawon and Mendut temples; Day 2: Prambanan temple complex, Kraton (Sultan's Palace); Day 3: Malioboro Street shopping, batik workshops, Taman Sari Water Castle. 2) Cultural Immersion (5 days) - adds Kasongan pottery village, traditional gamelan performances, Kalibiru National Park, and Jomblang Cave exploration. 3) Adventure Extension (7 days) - includes white-water rafting on Elo River, Merapi volcano trekking, Timang Beach gondola experience, and Pindul Cave tubing. Stay in the city center near Malioboro Street for convenience or Prawirotaman area for boutique hotels and cafes. Costs range from budget hostels (IDR 100,000-200,000/night) to boutique hotels (IDR 400,000-800,000/night) to luxury resorts (IDR 1,000,000-3,000,000/night). Transportation options include affordable taxis, ride-hailing apps (Grab/Gojek), rental scooters (IDR 70,000-100,000/day), or private car with driver (IDR 500,000-700,000/day). Visit during dry season (May-September) for optimal temple exploration.
