Egypt

Experience the birthplace of civilization with its ancient monuments, vibrant cities, and stunning desert landscapes along the legendary Nile River.

About Egypt

Egypt, officially the Arab Republic of Egypt, is a transcontinental country spanning the northeast corner of Africa and southwest corner of Asia. With one of the longest histories of any country, Egypt is considered a cradle of civilization. The country is best known for its ancient monuments such as the Giza Pyramid Complex and the Great Sphinx, but is also home to vibrant modern cities and beautiful coastal resorts. The Nile River, which flows north through the country into the Mediterranean Sea, has been the lifeline of Egyptian civilization since ancient times. Today, the majority of Egypt's population of over 100 million lives near the banks of the Nile. Cairo, the capital and largest city, is a bustling metropolis that blends ancient history with modern urban life. Egypt's culture is rich and diverse, influenced by its position at the crossroads of Africa, the Middle East, and the Mediterranean.

Top Attractions

Pyramids of Giza

Home to the Great Pyramid, the only surviving structure of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, alongside the Sphinx and other monuments.

Luxor

Ancient Thebes featuring the Valley of the Kings, Karnak Temple, and Luxor Temple, with magnificent monuments on both banks of the Nile.

Red Sea

World-class diving and snorkeling destinations like Sharm El-Sheikh and Hurghada, with crystal clear waters and vibrant coral reefs.

Cairo

Egypt's vibrant capital featuring the Egyptian Museum, Khan el-Khalili bazaar, and Islamic Cairo with its historic mosques and architecture.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Egyptian adventure

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Egypt is during the cooler months from October to April when temperatures are comfortable for sightseeing. Winter (December-February) is peak tourist season with mild days (15-24°C) but cool evenings and occasional rain. Spring (March-April) and autumn (October-November) offer warm days and fewer crowds. Summer (May-September) brings extreme heat (35-40°C), especially in Upper Egypt and desert areas, though coastal areas like Alexandria and Sinai offer relief with sea breezes. Ramadan dates vary each year and may affect opening hours and availability of services.

Transportation

Domestic flights connect major cities and tourist destinations, saving time when traveling between Cairo, Luxor, Aswan, and coastal resorts. Trains offer an affordable way to travel between cities along the Nile, with overnight sleeper trains available between Cairo and Luxor/Aswan. Nile cruises are a popular and scenic way to visit multiple ancient sites between Luxor and Aswan. In cities, use metered taxis or ride-hailing apps like Uber and Careem. The Cairo Metro is efficient for getting around the capital. For shorter distances, tuk-tuks operate in many cities and towns. Consider guided tours for visiting archaeological sites to enhance the experience with historical context.

Local Cuisine

Egyptian cuisine features flavorful dishes with influences from the Mediterranean, Middle East, and North Africa. Try koshari, the national dish combining rice, lentils, pasta, and tomato sauce with fried onions. Ful medames (mashed fava beans) and ta'ameya (Egyptian falafel made with fava beans) are popular breakfast items. Molokhia is a distinctive green soup made from jute leaves, typically served with chicken or rabbit. Grilled meats like kofta (spiced meatballs) and kebab are widely available. Fresh bread like aish baladi accompanies most meals. For dessert, try basbousa (semolina cake) or kunafa (shredded pastry with sweet cheese). Egyptian tea (shai) is served throughout the day, while fresh juice stands offer seasonal options.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly, especially at religious sites where shoulders and knees should be covered. Conservative attire is appreciated throughout Egypt, particularly for women. Learn basic Arabic phrases like "shukran" (thank you) and "salaam alaikum" (peace be upon you). When invited to a home, bringing a small gift is appreciated. Remove shoes before entering mosques, and women should cover their hair. Photography may be restricted at certain sites or require permits. Tipping (baksheesh) is customary for services. Public displays of affection should be limited. During Ramadan, avoid eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours. Handshaking is common in greetings, though men should wait for women to extend their hand first. Egyptians are known for their hospitality and helpfulness to visitors.

Egypt travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

What are the recommended tour options for traveling to Egypt?

Popular tour options include: 1) Group tours (8-15 people) by companies like Intrepid and G Adventures offering 7-14 day classic Egypt packages with the pyramids, Luxor, and Nile cruises ($800-1500). 2) Private guided tours providing customized itineraries with dedicated Egyptologists ($100-200/day). 3) Day tours in major cities for specific attractions ($30-100). 4) Nile cruises (3-7 days) between Luxor and Aswan ($400-1000). 5) Red Sea resort packages focusing on diving and beaches. During summer (June-August), choose morning tours to avoid extreme heat. Popular combined packages include Cairo-Luxor-Aswan routes and Cairo plus Red Sea extensions.

What should I consider when choosing a Nile cruise?

When selecting a Nile cruise, consider: 1) Duration - standard cruises run 3-4 nights between Luxor and Aswan; longer 7-night options include Lake Nasser. 2) Vessel category - ranging from 3-star ($80-120/night) to luxury 5-star ($200-400/night) boats. 3) Cabin location - higher decks offer better views but may have more motion. 4) Itinerary - standard routes include Luxor's Karnak Temple, Valley of the Kings, Edfu, Kom Ombo, and Aswan's Philae Temple. 5) Inclusions - check if shore excursions, guides, entrance fees, and transfers are included. 6) Sailing vs. motor - dahabiyas (traditional sailboats) offer intimate experiences for 8-12 passengers but cost more. Book 2-3 months ahead, or 6 months for winter high season.

What are the transportation options between Cairo and Luxor?

Transportation options between Cairo and Luxor (675km) include: 1) Flights - daily 1-hour flights by EgyptAir and other carriers ($80-150 one-way), the fastest and most comfortable option. 2) Overnight sleeper train - 10-hour journey with private cabins ($80-100) or seated tickets ($40-60). 3) Daytime trains - 9-10 hours on air-conditioned services ($20-30). 4) Bus - Egypt Go Bus and others offer 9-hour trips ($15-25) with less comfort. 5) Private drivers - door-to-door service for $150-250 with stops at attractions like Abydos Temple. Book train tickets 1-2 days ahead in person or through hotel services. During major holidays and festivals, book flights 2-3 weeks in advance.

Where can I exchange currency in Egypt?

Exchange options include: 1) Banks - offer official rates with longer processing times; open Sunday-Thursday 8:30am-2pm. 2) Official exchange offices - found in tourist areas and shopping malls with competitive rates and shorter lines. 3) ATMs - widely available in cities and tourist areas, dispensing Egyptian Pounds (EGP) with typical withdrawal limits of 2000-3000 EGP. 4) Hotels - convenient but with less favorable rates. 5) Airport exchanges - useful for initial funds but rates are 3-5% lower than in city centers. US dollars, euros, and British pounds are easiest to exchange. Many tourist establishments accept credit cards and major currencies directly. Avoid street money changers. The informal "baksheesh" tipping system makes having small denominations (5, 10, 20 EGP notes) essential.

What are the general entry requirements for Egypt?

Entry requirements include: 1) Passport valid for at least 6 months beyond arrival date with at least two blank pages. 2) Visa - available as an e-visa online ($25 single entry, $60 multiple entry), visa-on-arrival ($25, available to many nationalities at major airports), or from Egyptian embassies/consulates. 3) Return/onward ticket. 4) Proof of accommodation. 5) Sufficient funds for your stay. Some nationalities (mainly from Gulf countries) are exempt from visa requirements. Single-entry tourist visas are valid for 30 days. For Sinai resorts like Sharm El-Sheikh, a free 15-day entry stamp is available for many nationalities. No special vaccinations are currently required, though COVID requirements may change periodically.

What are airport transfer options in Aswan?

Aswan airport (ASW) transfer options include: 1) Pre-arranged hotel transfers - most reliable option, typically 150-200 EGP ($5-10). 2) Official airport taxis - white vehicles with set rates of approximately 150 EGP ($5-8) to central Aswan. 3) Ride-sharing - Uber and Careem operate in Aswan with slightly lower fares than taxis. 4) Public minibuses - cheapest option (5-10 EGP) but inconvenient with luggage and operate only on main routes. 5) Tour operator transfers - included in many package tours. The airport is small and located 16km southwest of the city, with a travel time of 20-30 minutes to most hotels. For early/late flights, arrange transportation in advance as options are limited during off-hours.

How do I apply for an E-visa for Egypt?

To apply for an Egyptian e-visa: 1) Visit the official portal (visa2egypt.gov.eg). 2) Create an account and complete the application form. 3) Upload a color scan of your passport's bio page and a recent digital photo (5x5 cm, white background). 4) Pay the fee online ($25 single-entry, $60 multiple-entry) using a credit/debit card. 5) Wait for processing (typically 3-7 business days). 6) Download and print your e-visa approval. The e-visa is valid for 90 days from issue date, allowing stays up to 30 days (single-entry) or 90 days (multiple-entry). Apply at least 7 days before travel. Ensure your passport is valid for at least 8 months from entry date. If your application is rejected (rare), you can still obtain a visa on arrival at Egyptian airports ($25).

What is the customary tipping etiquette in Egypt?

Tipping (baksheesh) is an essential part of Egyptian culture: 1) Hotel staff - 5-10 EGP for porters per bag, 5-10 EGP daily for housekeeping. 2) Restaurants - 10% in tourist establishments (even when service charge is included), 5-10% in local restaurants. 3) Guides - 100-150 EGP ($5-8) per day for group tours, 200-300 EGP ($10-15) for private guides. 4) Drivers - 50-100 EGP ($3-5) per day. 5) Bathroom attendants - 3-5 EGP. 6) Boat ride operators - 20-40 EGP. 7) Photography permissions at some sites or with camels/animals - 5-20 EGP. Keep small denominations (5, 10, 20 EGP) readily available. Tips are generally expected for any small service. When bargaining in markets, a small tip after purchase can maintain goodwill.
