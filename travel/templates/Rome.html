Rome

Explore the Eternal City, where ancient history and modern life blend seamlessly among magnificent ruins and vibrant piazzas.

About Rome

Rome, the capital of Italy, is a city with nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture, and culture on display. Ancient ruins such as the Roman Forum and the Colosseum evoke the power of the former Roman Empire. Vatican City, headquarters of the Roman Catholic Church, boasts St. Peter's Basilica and museums with masterpieces by Michelangelo and Raphael. Beyond its historical significance, modern Rome is a bustling metropolis with lively neighborhoods, delicious cuisine, and a vibrant street life that continues to captivate visitors from around the world.

Top Attractions

The Colosseum

Iconic amphitheater of ancient Rome, once hosting gladiatorial contests and public spectacles.

Vatican City

World's smallest independent state, home to St. Peter's Basilica and the Sistine Chapel.

Trevi Fountain

Baroque masterpiece and Rome's largest fountain, famed for the coin-tossing tradition.

Roman Forum

Sprawling ruins of ancient government buildings at the heart of the Roman Empire.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Roman adventure

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. Summer is hot and crowded, while winter offers the lowest prices.

Transportation

Use the Metro for long distances and buses for shorter trips. Consider a Roma Pass for public transportation and museum access. The historic center is best explored on foot.

Local Cuisine

Try authentic Roman pasta dishes like carbonara, cacio e pepe, and amatriciana. Visit trattorias away from tourist areas for better food at lower prices. Enjoy gelato as a refreshing treat.

Etiquette & Safety

Dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Be aware of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas. Remember that restaurants may charge a small cover fee (coperto) for table service.

Rome travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

What are the recommended areas to stay in Rome?

Recommended Rome neighborhoods: 1) Centro Storico (Historic Center) - heart of ancient Rome with Pantheon, Piazza Navona, and Trevi Fountain; premium location at €150-300/night; cobblestoned streets with limited transportation access but walking distance to major attractions. 2) Monti - trendy, bohemian area adjacent to the Colosseum; hip restaurants and boutiques; slightly more affordable at €120-220/night; excellent metro connections. 3) Trastevere - charming, authentic neighborhood across Tiber River with winding streets, excellent dining, and local atmosphere; limited public transportation; busier/noisier at night; €130-250/night. 4) Prati - elegant, upscale residential area near Vatican City; wider streets, good shopping, less touristy atmosphere; excellent metro connections; €120-200/night. 5) Testaccio - authentic, non-touristy neighborhood known for food scene; requires more public transit to sights; €90-180/night. 6) Termini/Repubblica - convenient for transportation but less atmospheric; budget options from €70-150/night. Best suited for first-time visitors: Centro Storico or Monti. For longer stays or returning visitors: Trastevere or Prati. For budget constraints: Testaccio or San Lorenzo (university district).

What are the options for food tours in Rome?

Rome food tour options: 1) Trastevere food tours - explore this foodie neighborhood with companies like Eating Italy or Roman Food Tour (€85-110, 3-4 hours); includes 6-8 stops for traditional dishes, wine, and gelato. 2) Testaccio Market tours - visit Rome's most authentic food market with tastings at local establishments; offered by Eating Europe or Casa Mia (€75-95, 4 hours). 3) Pizza and pasta making classes - hands-on experiences with chefs teaching traditional techniques; popular providers include Walks of Italy and BonAppetour (€60-120, 2-3 hours). 4) Evening food and wine walks - combines dinner with sightseeing in Centro Storico or Monti; LivItaly and Devour Tours offer well-reviewed options (€90-130, 3-4 hours). 5) Private customized tours - tailored experiences with personal guides for dietary preferences or specific cuisines (€120-250 per person). 6) Self-guided options - downloadable itineraries from GPSmyCity or Lonely Planet (€5-15). Book 2-3 days ahead in low season, 1-2 weeks during high season (April-October). Most tours accommodate dietary restrictions with advance notice. Morning market tours offer fresher food; evening tours include more wine and lively atmosphere.

What are the public transportation options in Rome?

Rome public transportation options: 1) Metro system - simplest but limited network with 3 lines (A, B, B1, and C); runs 5:30am-11:30pm (Friday/Saturday until 1:30am); single ticket €1.50 valid for 100 minutes. 2) Buses - extensive network covering the entire city; key tourist lines include 40, 64 (Termini-Vatican), 75 (Colosseum-Aventine Hill), 170 (Termini-Trastevere); same tickets as metro. 3) Trams - 6 lines operating in less central areas; quieter and more reliable than buses; same ticketing system. 4) 24/48/72-hour tourist passes - unlimited travel for €7/€12.50/€18 respectively. 5) Weekly pass (CIS) - €24 for 7 calendar days. Purchase tickets at metro stations, tobacco shops (tabacchi), newsstands, or via MyClip app. Validate paper tickets in yellow machines upon first use. Be aware of pickpockets, especially on crowded bus routes 40 and 64. During summer, buses can be very hot and crowded. Metro Line A connects most major tourist sites (Spanish Steps, Vatican, Trevi Fountain area), while Line B serves Colosseum and Circus Maximus. For late night travel, night buses (marked with "N") operate after metro closure but with limited frequency.

What are some recommended day trips from Rome?

Popular day trips from Rome: 1) Pompeii/Naples - 1.5-hour high-speed train to Naples (€30-50 one-way), then local train to Pompeii (30 min, €3); guided tours recommended for Pompeii. 2) Tivoli Gardens - Villa d'Este (Renaissance gardens with fountains) and Hadrian's Villa (ancient Roman complex); accessible by train (€3, 40 min) or organized tours (€60-85). 3) Ostia Antica - ancient Rome's port city with well-preserved ruins, less crowded than Pompeii; 30-minute train ride (€1.50); entry €12. 4) Castel Gandolfo - papal summer residence with beautiful lake; 45-minute train ride (€2.10); Vatican Museums offers combined ticket with gardens. 5) Orvieto - Etruscan hill town with magnificent cathedral; 1-hour train ride (€8-15); access by funicular from station. 6) Florence - feasible as long day trip via high-speed train (1.5 hours, €40-80 one-way); book Uffizi/Accademia in advance. 7) Amalfi Coast - long but popular day tour (12-14 hours, €150-200) by organized tour only. For independent travel, check Trenitalia or Italo train schedules and book ahead for best prices. Most organized tours include hotel pickup and average €80-150 per person with transportation and guide. Summer months require advance booking, especially for Pompeii and Florence trips.

What guided tour options are available for the Colosseum?

Colosseum guided tour options: 1) Official Colosseum tours - run by Parco Colosseo with English available; basic tour (€35) includes third tier and arena floor; underground tour (€57) adds subterranean chambers where gladiators and animals waited. 2) Skip-the-line group tours - offered by companies like Walks of Italy, City Wonders, and GetYourGuide; typically include Roman Forum and Palatine Hill; average €50-65 for 3 hours in groups of 20-25 people. 3) Small group premium tours (6-12 people) - more personalized experience with additional access areas; €75-90 for 3-4 hours. 4) Private guides - customizable pace and content; €250-350 for 3 hours for up to 4 people. 5) Evening tours - limited availability (summer only) to see Colosseum illuminated with smaller crowds; €80-100. 6) Virtual reality tours - combine traditional guide with digital reconstructions showing how the Colosseum looked during ancient times; €15-20 supplement to regular ticket. 7) Self-guided audio tours - rental from venue (€6) or downloadable apps like Rick Steves Audio Europe (free). Morning tours (8-10am) or later afternoon (3-5pm) offer fewer crowds. Book at least 1-2 weeks ahead during peak season (March-October) as special access areas sell out quickly.

How can I purchase tickets for the Colosseum?

Colosseum ticket purchasing options: 1) Official website (coopculture.it) - basic entrance ticket (€18) includes Roman Forum and Palatine Hill; valid for 24 hours; timed entry required. 2) Roma Pass - 2-day (€32) or 3-day (€52) city pass includes one or two free museum entries (Colosseum can be one) plus public transportation; available online or at tourist information points. 3) Guided tour packages - include ticket plus guide service; range from €45-80 depending on group size and areas accessed; available through GetYourGuide, Viator, or Walks of Italy. 4) Combo tickets - Full Experience ticket (€22) adds access to SUPER sites (special archeological areas). 5) On-site purchase - possible but not recommended due to long lines (1-2 hours in peak season). 6) Tourist information offices - located at Termini Station, Castel Sant'Angelo, and Via dei Fori Imperiali. Tickets include timed entry for Colosseum only; entry to Forum/Palatine is flexible same day. Book at least 2-3 weeks ahead for high season (April-October) or for underground/arena floor access. Children under 18 enter free (ID required) but still need reservation. Mobile tickets accepted, save confirmation email and code for entry. Combined Vatican/Colosseum tickets available through third-party sites incur surcharges but save booking separately.

What are the best hotel options in Rome?

Rome hotel recommendations by category: 1) Luxury options - Hotel de Russie (€500-900) near Spanish Steps offers elegant rooms and central garden; Hotel Hassler (€450-800) at top of Spanish Steps provides excellent views; J.K. Place Roma (€500-800) offers boutique luxury near shopping district. 2) Mid-range boutique hotels - Hotel Campo de' Fiori (€150-300) in historic center; Hotel Artemide (€140-250) near Termini with excellent service; Fifteen Keys (€180-280) in Monti district offering contemporary design. 3) Budget-friendly choices - Generator Rome (€60-120) modern hostel with private rooms; Hotel Paba (€90-140) near Colosseum; Hotel des Artistes (€80-150) clean, reliable option near Termini. 4) Family-friendly options - Albergo del Senato (€200-400) spacious family rooms near Pantheon; Hotel Maggiore (€140-220) offers quad rooms and connecting options; Rome Cavalieri (€280-450) outside center with resort-style pool facilities. 5) Apartments - Rome Unique Home (€120-250) offers serviced apartments in central areas; CrossPolitan Rome (€100-200) provides budget apartments with kitchenettes. Book 3-4 months ahead for April-June and September-October high seasons. Request quiet rooms facing courtyards rather than streets, especially in Centro Storico and Trastevere. Many historic buildings lack elevators; specify if mobility issues exist.

What are the train travel options between Rome and Venice?

Rome-Venice train options: 1) High-speed trains (Frecciarossa, Italo) - fastest option taking 3.5-4 hours; 15+ daily departures from Roma Termini to Venezia Santa Lucia (island station) or Venezia Mestre (mainland); fares range from €29.90-49.90 (super economy) to €89-120 (standard/flexible); advance purchase highly recommended. 2) Intercity trains - slower service (6+ hours) with 1-2 daily connections; slightly cheaper at €27-70 but rarely worth the time trade-off. 3) Night train (Intercity Notte) - departs Rome around 11:30pm, arrives Venice 6:30am; sleeper cabins (€130-200 for private compartment) or seated options (€40-60); good for budget travelers maximizing sightseeing time. Both major operators (Trenitalia and Italo) offer comparable service; Italo sometimes has lower promotional fares. Book 2-3 months ahead for best prices; fares increase significantly closer to travel date. Roma Termini and Venezia Santa Lucia stations offer storage services for luggage (€6-8 per bag for 5 hours). First class (1st/Executive/Business) offers wider seats and complimentary drinks/snacks. Validate paper tickets before boarding (electronic tickets exempt). Punctuality generally excellent on high-speed routes. Scenic portions of journey include Tuscan countryside and crossing the Venice lagoon upon arrival.
