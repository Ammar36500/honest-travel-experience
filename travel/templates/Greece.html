About Greece

Greece is a country in southeastern Europe with thousands of islands throughout the Aegean and Ionian seas. It's considered the cradle of Western civilization, being the birthplace of democracy, Western philosophy, the Olympic Games, Western literature, historiography, political science, major scientific and mathematical principles, and Western drama.

Greece travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

How can I travel between the Greek islands?

Greek island transportation options include: 1) Ferries - the primary method with conventional (slower, cheaper) and high-speed (faster, pricier) options connecting most islands. Major operators include Blue Star, SeaJets, and Hellenic Seaways. 2) Flights - Olympic/Aegean Airlines offer connections between larger islands and Athens; Sky Express serves smaller islands. 3) Island hopping packages - pre-arranged ferry/accommodation combinations. Book ferries through FerryHopper.com or individual company websites. High season (June-September) requires booking 1-2 months ahead. The Cyclades and Saronic Gulf islands have frequent connections; the Dodecanese and North Aegean islands have more limited schedules. Consider weather conditions, as winter services are reduced and rough seas can cause cancellations.

Which Greek island should I visit for a beach vacation?

For beach vacations: 1) Crete - offers diverse beaches from pink-sand Elafonisi to palm-lined Vai and family-friendly resorts. 2) Zakynthos - features the famous Navagio (Shipwreck) Beach and clear waters ideal for swimming. 3) Milos - known for unique volcanic formations and over 70 beaches including Sarakiniko's white moonscape. 4) Corfu - provides sandy beaches with excellent facilities and greenery. 5) Rhodes - combines historical sites with developed beach resorts and reliable sunshine. 6) Naxos - offers long, sandy beaches with shallow waters perfect for families. 7) Mykonos - features beautiful beaches with a party atmosphere and upscale amenities. The best beach season runs May-October, with July-August being hottest and most crowded. September offers warm seas with fewer crowds.

What are some family-friendly itinerary suggestions for Greece?

Family-friendly itineraries: 1) Athens (3 days) + Naxos (5 days) - combine the Acropolis and child-friendly museums with Naxos's shallow beaches and waterparks. 2) Corfu (7 days) - all-inclusive resorts, water activities, and Aqualand waterpark. 3) Crete (10 days) - diverse activities including beaches, the family-friendly Acqua Plus waterpark, and interactive archaeological sites like Knossos. 4) Rhodes (5 days) + Kos (3 days) - medieval castles, beaches, and easy ferry connections. 5) Peloponnese road trip (7 days) - ancient Olympia, Nafplio's beaches, and castle explorations with minimal ferry travel. Best family travel time is May-June or September when temperatures are milder (25-28°C) and crowds smaller. Many hotels offer family rooms or apartments with kitchenettes, and larger islands have medical facilities suitable for traveling with children.

What are the ferry options between the Cyclades islands?

Cyclades ferry options: 1) High-speed catamarans (SeaJets, Golden Star) - fastest option (2hrs Athens-Mykonos) but 30-50% more expensive (€60-90) and subject to weather cancellations. 2) Conventional ferries (Blue Star) - more stable, comfortable, allow deck access, and cheaper (€35-60) but slower (4.5hrs Athens-Mykonos). Popular routes include the Mykonos-Santorini-Ios-Paros-Naxos circuit with daily high season connections. Less visited islands like Folegandros or Amorgos have less frequent service (2-3 weekly). The main Athens departure ports are Piraeus (all destinations) and Rafina (closer for Mykonos/Andros). Book 1-3 months ahead for summer travel through FerryHopper.com. Island hopping passes like Eurail's Greek Islands Pass can save money on multiple journeys. Most ferries accommodate vehicles, but advance reservations are essential.

What are the must-visit places in Greece?

Must-visit Greek destinations: 1) Athens - the Acropolis, Parthenon, Acropolis Museum, and Plaka neighborhood (2-3 days). 2) Santorini - famous for whitewashed villages perched on volcanic cliffs, sunset views from Oia, and distinctive beaches (3-4 days). 3) Mykonos - combines beautiful beaches with vibrant nightlife and the picturesque Chora (town) with its windmills (2-3 days). 4) Crete - Greece's largest island featuring the Minoan palace of Knossos, stunning gorges, and beautiful beaches (5-7 days). 5) Delphi - ancient sanctuary with spectacular mountain setting, accessible as a day trip from Athens. 6) Meteora - monasteries dramatically perched atop towering rock formations (1-2 days). 7) Rhodes - medieval Old Town (UNESCO World Heritage site) with Byzantine and Ottoman influences. 8) Zakynthos - home to the iconic Shipwreck Beach and Blue Caves. Allow 10-14 days minimum to experience several of these highlights.

What are the car rental options at Athens Airport?

Athens Airport car rental options include: 1) International agencies (Hertz, Avis, Enterprise, Sixt, Budget) with counters at the arrivals level. 2) Local companies (Greek Budget, Avance, Caldera) offering competitive rates. Economy cars start at €30-40/day in low season and €60-80/day in summer. Book 2-3 months ahead for July-August. Requirements include: valid driver's license (International Driving Permit recommended for non-EU licenses), credit card in driver's name, age 21+ (25+ for larger vehicles, with surcharges for drivers under 25). Compact cars are recommended for narrow Greek roads and limited parking. Most rentals are manual transmission; specify automatic if needed (costs €10-15 extra per day). Consider insurance options carefully as basic CDW often has high deductibles (€700-1000). One-way rentals between Athens and islands incur significant surcharges (€150-300) plus ferry costs.

What are recommended accommodations in Santorini?

Santorini accommodation recommendations: 1) Oia - offers premium caldera-view hotels like Canaves Oia and Mystique (€350-800/night) with iconic sunset views. 2) Fira/Firostefani - centrally located with Aria Suites and Cosmopolitan Suites (€200-500/night) providing caldera views with more nightlife options. 3) Imerovigli - quieter village with Grace Santorini and Astra Suites (€250-600/night) featuring infinity pools overlooking the caldera. 4) Kamari/Perissa - beachfront locations with more affordable options like Tamarix Del Mar and Meltemi Village (€100-250/night). 5) Pyrgos - inland village offering authentic experience with boutique hotels like Carpe Diem (€150-300/night). Book 4-6 months in advance for cliff-side hotels with caldera views, especially for June-September. Many Santorini hotels are adults-only or have minimum age requirements. Budget travelers should consider inland villages like Karterados or Messaria where rooms start around €80/night.

What should I know about ferry travel in Greece?

Greek ferry essentials: 1) Book tickets through FerryHopper.com, Danae Travel, or official ferry websites 1-3 months ahead for summer travel. 2) Arrive 45-60 minutes before departure; boarding typically closes 15 minutes prior. 3) Ferry types include slower conventional vessels (Blue Star), faster catamarans (SeaJets), and hydrofoils for shorter routes. 4) Seating options range from general deck (cheapest) to airline-style reserved seats to private cabins on longer overnight routes. 5) Most routes operate daily in summer (June-September) but reduce to 2-3 weekly in winter with more cancellations due to weather, especially for smaller islands. 6) Major ports like Piraeus can be confusing - check your gate number ("Πύλη" in Greek) carefully as distances between gates can exceed 1km. 7) Ferry punctuality varies; delays of 30-60 minutes are common. 8) Bring motion sickness remedies for high-speed vessels and rough weather conditions, particularly in the windy Cyclades region.
