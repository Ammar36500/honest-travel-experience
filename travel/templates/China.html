About China

Home to 1.4 billion people, China blends 5,000 years of history with modern innovation. Visitors experience diverse landscapes from the Great Wall to Guilin's karst mountains, along with distinct regional cuisines and cultural traditions across a vast country of varied terrains and climates.

Top Attractions

Great Wall

The world's longest wall and largest ancient architecture, stretching over 13,000 miles across China's northern territories.

Forbidden City

Imperial palace complex in Beijing featuring 980 buildings with stunning traditional Chinese architecture.

Terracotta Army

Thousands of life-sized clay soldiers, horses, and chariots buried with Emperor Qin Shi Huang to protect him in the afterlife.

Li River, Guilin

Dramatic limestone karst landscape along the Li River, featured on the 20 Yuan banknote and countless traditional paintings.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Chinese adventure

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer the most comfortable temperatures and fewer crowds. Avoid Chinese national holidays, especially Chinese New Year (January/February) and National Day (October 1-7), when domestic tourism peaks and transportation is crowded. Northern China experiences cold winters and hot summers, while southern regions are generally warmer with summer monsoons.

Transportation

China has an extensive high-speed rail network connecting major cities, offering comfortable and affordable transportation. The Beijing-Shanghai line can cover 819 miles in just 4.5 hours. Major cities have efficient subway systems with English signage. Use DiDi (similar to Uber) for taxis in urban areas. Consider domestic flights for longer distances, as China is a vast country. Download translation apps, as English is not widely spoken outside major tourist areas.

Local Cuisine

Try regional specialties: spicy Sichuan dishes in Chengdu, dim sum in Hong Kong and Guangdong, dumplings and Beijing duck in the north, and fresh seafood along coastal areas. Street food offers authentic flavors at affordable prices, but stick to busy stalls with high turnover. Tea houses provide a traditional experience with various local teas. Most restaurants have picture menus or English translations in tourist areas. Tipping is not customary in most establishments.

Cultural Etiquette

Learn basic Mandarin phrases like "nǐ hǎo" (hello) and "xièxie" (thank you). When receiving business cards or gifts, use both hands as a sign of respect. Avoid discussing sensitive political topics in public. Remove shoes when entering someone's home. Pointing with your index finger is considered rude; use your open hand instead. Many Chinese people appreciate foreigners trying chopsticks, even if you're not skilled with them. Mobile payment via WeChat Pay and Alipay is ubiquitous, so consider setting these up if staying long-term.

China travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

What are common payment methods in China?

Mobile payments dominate in China, with WeChat Pay and Alipay used everywhere from restaurants to street vendors. Foreigners can now link international credit cards to WeChat Pay's international version. Cash is still accepted but increasingly less convenient. Major credit cards (Visa/Mastercard) work mainly at international hotels and upscale venues. Carry some cash (2000-3000 RMB) for emergencies. ATMs accepting foreign cards are available in major cities, particularly at international banks like HSBC.

What are the rules for China's transit visa?

China offers 24/72/144-hour visa-free transit for citizens of 53 eligible countries. Requirements include: having confirmed onward tickets to a third country departing within the allowed timeframe, staying within the permitted administrative area of your entry point, and entering/exiting through designated ports. Not all entry points offer the same transit duration. Declare your intention to use this policy at the visa-free transit counter upon arrival. This policy applies only for genuine transit purposes and may be suspended during special events or emergencies.

What are the transportation options between major Chinese cities?

High-speed trains are the premier option, connecting major cities at speeds up to 350km/h. Popular routes include Beijing-Shanghai (4.5 hours) and Beijing-Xi'an (5.5 hours). Book through the 12306 website/app or at stations using your passport. Domestic flights connect all major cities through airlines like Air China and budget carriers like Spring Airlines. For shorter distances, intercity buses are available. Within cities, 40+ Chinese cities have subway systems with English signage. For local transportation, DiDi (Chinese Uber) offers an English interface and accepts international cards.

What are the general visa requirements for China?

Most visitors need a tourist visa (L visa) obtained before arrival, requiring a passport valid for 6+ months, application form, photo, proof of accommodation, and round-trip itinerary. Single-entry tourist visas typically allow 30-day stays and cost $100-140 for US citizens. Application takes 4-7 business days. Exemptions include the 72/144-hour transit policy for 53 countries, visa-free entry for citizens of Singapore, Brunei, and Japan, and special policies for Hainan province. Apply through Chinese embassies or consulates. Visa policies change frequently, so verify current requirements before travel.

Where can I purchase a SIM card for use in China?

Purchase options include: 1) Before arrival through international travel SIM providers like SIMOptions. 2) At official carrier stores (China Mobile, China Unicom, China Telecom) in airports and city centers; bring your passport as registration is mandatory. Tourist packages cost 100-200 RMB ($15-30) for 30 days with 10-30GB data. China Unicom offers "CUniq" tourist SIMs with English support. Alternative options include pocket WiFi rental services or using international roaming (expensive). Ensure your phone is unlocked and compatible with Chinese networks. Most foreign apps and social media require a VPN to work in China.

What are popular train travel routes in China?

Popular routes include Beijing-Shanghai (4.5 hours, 550 RMB), Beijing-Xi'an (5.5 hours), and Shanghai-Hangzhou (1 hour). Scenic journeys include the Chengdu-Lhasa "Sky Road" (requires Tibet permits), Lanzhou-Urumqi through the Gobi Desert, and Beijing-Harbin. High-speed trains offer Second Class, First Class, and Business Class seating. Overnight trains provide Hard Sleeper (6-bunk open compartments) and Soft Sleeper (4-bunk private compartments) options. Book 30 days in advance for popular routes through the 12306 website/app or at station ticket offices, especially during holidays.

How can foreigners use WeChat Pay in China?

Foreigners can use WeChat Pay by: 1) Linking international credit cards directly to the international version of WeChat (navigate to "Me" > "Pay" > "Cards" > "Add Credit Card") with a 90-day usage limit. 2) Using the WeChat Pay tourist version, which allows linking international cards for 90 days with a 10,000 RMB transaction limit. 3) Opening a Chinese bank account and linking it to WeChat for full functionality (requires passport and residence permit or address proof). Alternative options include using international payment platforms like Swapi that integrate with WeChat. Always carry some cash as backup.

What are some recommended itineraries for traveling in China?

First-time visitors: 10-14 day "Golden Triangle" covering Beijing (4 days: Great Wall, Forbidden City), Xi'an (3 days: Terracotta Warriors), and Shanghai (3 days: The Bund, Yu Garden). With more time, add a Yangtze River cruise and Chengdu for pandas. Nature enthusiasts: 2-week "Southern Scenic Route" covering Guilin/Yangshuo, Zhangjiajie, Huangshan, and Hangzhou. Culture seekers: 2-week "Silk Road Journey" through Xi'an, Dunhuang, and Kashgar. Best travel times are spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) when temperatures are moderate. Combine high-speed trains for shorter legs with flights for longer distances.
