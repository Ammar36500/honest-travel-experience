Switzerland

Experience the breathtaking beauty of the Alps, pristine lakes, and charming villages in this Alpine paradise.

About Switzerland

Switzerland is a small Alpine country in Central Europe known for its stunning mountain landscapes, crystal-clear lakes, and picturesque villages. Famous for its neutrality, banking system, chocolate, cheese, and watchmaking, Switzerland consistently ranks among the world's most livable countries with one of the highest standards of living.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Swiss adventure

Best Time to Visit

June to August for hiking, December to March for skiing, and April-May or September-October for fewer crowds.

Transportation

Consider the Swiss Travel Pass for unlimited travel on trains, buses, and boats. Swiss public transport is famously punctual and extensive.

Local Cuisine

Try fondue, raclette, rösti, and Swiss chocolate. Each region has its own culinary specialties influenced by neighboring countries.

Budget Planning

Switzerland is expensive - budget accordingly. Look for tax-free shopping opportunities and consider staying in mountain huts.

Switzerland travel advice

These questions are the most asked questions and are answerd here.

What are some family-friendly activities in Switzerland?

Family-friendly Switzerland activities: 1) Mountain excursions - Jungfraujoch has snow activities year-round plus Ice Palace; Mt. Pilatus offers toboggan runs and Switzerland's longest summer sledge ride; Mt. Titlis features revolving cable car and glacier park. 2) Swiss Museum of Transport (Lucerne) - interactive exhibits, planetarium, and chocolate adventure. 3) Adventure parks - Seilpark Interlaken, Rope Park Pilatus, and Forest Fun Park Zermatt offer ziplines and rope courses for various ages. 4) Chocolate experiences - visit Maison Cailler factory in Broc or Lindt Home of Chocolate near Zurich with interactive exhibits and tastings. 5) Alpine wildlife parks - Goldau Nature Park and Zoo Zurich allow close encounters with local animals. 6) Rhine Falls boat rides - Europe's largest waterfall with family-friendly vessel options. 7) Mountain coasters - Fräkigaudi (Engelberg), Oeschinensee, and Pfingstegg (Grindelwald) offer thrilling rides down alpine slopes. 8) Indoor options for rainy days include Swiss Science Center Technorama (Winterthur) and Aquaparc (Le Bouveret). Most mountain destinations offer family passes with significant discounts. Children under 6 typically travel free on public transportation when accompanied by a paying adult; ages 6-16 qualify for reduced "Junior" fares or free travel with a Junior Card (CHF 30 annually).

What are some recommended itineraries for Switzerland?

Recommended Switzerland itineraries: 1) Classic 7-day tour - Zurich (1 day), Lucerne (2 days) including Mt. Pilatus/Rigi, Interlaken/Jungfrau Region (2 days) for Jungfraujoch excursion, Zermatt (1 day) for Matterhorn views, Geneva or Montreux (1 day). 2) Scenic 10-day Grand Train Tour - combine panoramic routes including Glacier Express, Bernina Express, GoldenPass, and Gotthard Panorama Express; start in Zurich and visit St. Gallen, Davos, St. Moritz, Zermatt, Montreux, Interlaken, and Lucerne. 3) Alpine adventure (5-7 days) - focus on Bernese Oberland with stays in Interlaken, Grindelwald, or Wengen; hike Männlichen to Kleine Scheidegg, visit Jungfraujoch, and explore Lauterbrunnen Valley. 4) Swiss-Italian lakes (6-8 days) - combine Lake Lugano and Lake Como visits with stops in Bellinzona (UNESCO castles) and Locarno; add Bernina Express journey. 5) Winter sports week - base in Zermatt, Verbier, St. Moritz, or Davos-Klosters for world-class skiing and winter activities. Best seasons: Summer (June-August) for hiking and outdoor activities; September for fewer crowds and pleasant weather; December-March for winter sports. Consider Swiss Travel Pass for itineraries visiting multiple regions; accommodations should be booked 3-6 months in advance for summer high season.

Which Swiss rail pass should I choose for travel?

Swiss rail pass options: 1) Swiss Travel Pass - comprehensive coverage of trains, buses, boats, and most mountain railways; includes museum entry; available for 3, 4, 6, 8, or 15 consecutive days (CHF 232-429, 2nd class); ideal for travelers visiting multiple regions within limited timeframe. 2) Swiss Travel Pass Flex - same benefits as regular pass but for non-consecutive days (3, 4, 6, 8, or 15 days within one month); approximately 15% more expensive but offers flexibility. 3) Swiss Half Fare Card - 50% discount on all public transportation for one month (CHF 120); cost-effective if making fewer journeys or traveling over longer period. 4) Regional passes - Bernese Oberland Pass or Tell Pass offer unlimited travel within specific regions; good for concentrated stays. 5) Swiss Family Card - free for children under 16 traveling with parents holding any Swiss Travel Pass. Decision factors: Calculate point when pass becomes economical (typically 3-4 major journeys); consider if your itinerary includes expensive mountain excursions (Jungfraujoch discount only 25%); factor in incidental transportation and museum entries. First-class passes cost approximately 50% more than second class. All passes except Half Fare Card include urban public transportation. Purchase online before arrival for best prices. For travelers visiting primarily one base without extensive travel, individual tickets or regional passes may be more economical.

What are the best scenic train routes in Switzerland?

Switzerland's top scenic train routes: 1) Glacier Express (8 hours) - connects St. Moritz and Zermatt through 91 tunnels and over 291 bridges with panoramic windows; reservation required (CHF 33) plus regular ticket/rail pass; known as world's slowest express train. 2) Bernina Express (4 hours) - UNESCO World Heritage route from Chur/St. Moritz to Tirano (Italy) crossing highest alpine railway crossing; famous for Landwasser Viaduct and circular viaduct at Brusio; reservations needed (CHF 14-26). 3) GoldenPass Line (5.5 hours) - connects Lucerne to Montreux via Interlaken with three different trains; no reservations required except for panoramic carriages; classic scenery from Central Switzerland to Lake Geneva. 4) Gotthard Panorama Express (5.5 hours) - combines boat ride on Lake Lucerne with train journey through historic Gotthard route; operates April-October; reservation required (CHF 16) plus ticket/pass. 5) Centovalli Railway (2 hours) - narrow-gauge line connecting Locarno (Switzerland) to Domodossola (Italy) through "hundred valleys"; palm trees transition to alpine scenes. Best seasons: spring for green valleys and wildflowers; autumn for foliage; winter for snow-covered landscapes (though some routes have reduced service). Morning departures usually offer clearer mountain views before afternoon clouds develop.

What should I know about the Glacier Express in Switzerland?

Glacier Express essentials: 1) Route connects Zermatt and St. Moritz (or Davos) in approximately 8 hours, crossing 291 bridges and 91 tunnels through spectacular alpine scenery. 2) Operates year-round with 1-2 daily departures in each direction; reduced service in shoulder seasons. 3) Reservation is mandatory (CHF 33 in peak season, CHF 23 off-peak) in addition to regular train ticket or rail pass. 4) Seating classes include 2nd class (comfortable), 1st class (more spacious), and Excellence Class (premium service with guaranteed window seats, 5-course meal, tablet guide, and dedicated concierge for CHF 420 supplement). 5) Full three-course lunch served at seat, pre-ordering recommended (CHF 42-48); simpler options available from bistro car. 6) Panoramic carriages feature oversized windows extending into roof portions for mountain views. 7) Commentary available via headphones or app in multiple languages. 8) Most scenic sections include Oberalp Pass (highest point at 2,033m), Rhine Gorge ("Swiss Grand Canyon"), and Landwasser Viaduct. 9) Entire journey not necessary - popular partial routes include Zermatt-Andermatt or Chur-St. Moritz. 10) Book 2-3 months ahead for summer peak season (July-August) or Christmas/New Year periods. Travel tip: For priority window seats, reserve early and choose right side when traveling Zermatt to St. Moritz or left side in opposite direction.

What should I know about renting a car in Switzerland?

Switzerland car rental information: 1) Requirements - valid driver's license (International Driving Permit recommended but not mandatory for most nationalities), minimum age 20 (25 for premium vehicles), major credit card. 2) Major companies include Hertz, Avis, Europcar, Budget, and local provider Mobility; rates from CHF 70-120/day for economy cars. 3) Manual transmission is standard; automatic costs approximately 15-25% more and requires advance reservation. 4) Winter driving (November-April) in mountain areas requires winter tires (included by rental companies during season) and sometimes snow chains (usually available for additional fee). 5) Swiss motorway system requires annual vignette sticker (CHF 40) already included with rentals. 6) Fuel costs approximately CHF 1.80-2.00/liter (gasoline) and CHF 1.90-2.10/liter (diesel); rentals must be returned with same fuel level. 7) Parking in cities is expensive (CHF 3-5/hour) and limited; hotels charge CHF 15-35/night. 8) Mountain passes may close due to snow even in summer; check conditions at TCS.ch before travel. 9) Alternative to traditional rentals: Mobility car-sharing available at most train stations with hourly rates (requires registration). Consider whether car is necessary given Switzerland's excellent public transportation; rental most valuable for exploring rural areas away from rail lines or flexibility with young children.

What are the benefits of the Swiss Travel Pass?

Swiss Travel Pass benefits: 1) Unlimited travel on the comprehensive network of trains, buses, and boats operated by SBB and other companies. 2) Free travel on public transportation in 90+ cities and towns. 3) Free admission to 500+ museums nationwide, including major attractions like the Swiss National Museum and Château de Chillon. 4) 50% discount on most mountain railways and cable cars (including Rigi, Pilatus, and Schilthorn; only 25% on Jungfraujoch and Matterhorn Glacier Paradise). 5) Free mountain excursions on Rigi, Stanserhorn, and Stoos. 6) Covered scenic routes including GoldenPass Line, Gotthard Panorama Express, and Luzern-Interlaken Express (reservation fees extra for Glacier Express and Bernina Express). 7) Swiss Family Card included free with adult passes, allowing children under 16 to travel free when accompanied by a parent. 8) Available in consecutive day versions (3, 4, 6, 8, or 15 days) or Flex version for non-consecutive days within one month. Price ranges from CHF 232 (3-day, 2nd class) to CHF 429 (15-day, 2nd class); 1st class costs approximately 50% more. Most cost-effective for travelers visiting multiple regions, making several long-distance journeys, or planning multiple mountain excursions. Purchase before arrival for slightly better rates and convenience. Digital and paper versions available.

What are the train travel options from Zurich Airport?

Zurich Airport train connections: 1) Direct trains to Zurich Main Station (Hauptbahnhof) run every 5-10 minutes, journey time 10 minutes, CHF 6.80 one-way (included with Swiss Travel Pass). 2) Direct intercity connections to major Swiss cities without changing trains: Basel (1h15), Bern (1h10), Lucerne (1h), Geneva (3h), Lausanne (2h20), St. Gallen (1h), and Interlaken (2h). 3) International direct services to Stuttgart (3h30), Munich (4h), and Vienna (8h). 4) Train station location in underground level of airport between Terminals 1 and 2, well-signed from arrivals. 5) Ticket purchase options include SBB counter with English-speaking staff (in arrival hall), self-service machines (multiple languages), or SBB mobile app (requires registration). 6) First trains depart around 5am, last trains between 11pm-12am depending on destination. 7) All platforms accessible by elevator with facilities for luggage and disabled passengers. 8) Airport trains have dedicated luggage storage areas. 9) Alternative to trains: Bus services to destinations not directly connected by rail, including Flixbus for international journeys. 10) Taxis available but expensive (CHF 60-70 to Zurich center, CHF 400+ to distant locations like Zermatt). Pre-purchasing tickets online or via app offers small discounts on some intercity routes.
